Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) Questions

What is the primary responsibility of a CNA?
A) Diagnosing medical conditions
B) Assisting patients with activities of daily living (ADLs)
C) Prescribing medication
D) Performing surgical procedures
CNAs are trained to provide direct patient care, which includes helping with ADLs like bathing, dressing, and eating. Diagnosis, prescription, and surgery are roles for licensed nurses and physicians.
The law that protects patient confidentiality is known as:
A) OSHA
B) FDA
C) HIPAA
D) CDC
The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) sets the standard for protecting sensitive patient health information.
Which of the following is an example of objective information?
A) The patient says they feel dizzy.
B) The patient's blood pressure is 120/80 mmHg.
C) The patient's wife says he was confused.
D) The patient complains of a headache.
Objective data is measurable and observable (a fact), like a vital sign. Subjective data is what the patient reports or feels (a symptom).
What is the single most important way to prevent the spread of infection?
A) Wearing gloves.
B) Proper handwashing.
C) Using a face mask.
D) Isolating the patient.
While other methods are important, handwashing is the most effective action to remove pathogens and prevent their transmission from person to person.
The acronym "RACE" in fire safety stands for:
A) Run, Activate, Call, Evacuate
B) Rescue, Alarm, Contain, Extinguish/Evacuate
C) Rush, Alert, Confine, Exit
D) Remove, Activate, Call, Exit
RACE is a universal acronym for fire response: Rescue anyone in immediate danger, pull the Alarm, Contain the fire by closing doors, and Extinguish if safe or Evacuate.
When assisting a patient with bathing, what is the most important principle to follow?
A) Use very hot water to ensure cleanliness.
B) Maintain the patient's privacy and dignity.
C) Bathe the patient as quickly as possible.
D) Always use scented soaps.
Maintaining privacy and dignity is paramount in all aspects of patient care, especially during personal hygiene tasks.
Which of the following is considered a normal adult oral temperature?
A) 96.8°F (36.0°C)
B) 98.6°F (37.0°C)
C) 100.4°F (38.0°C)
D) 95.0°F (35.0°C)
98.6°F (37.0°C) is the average normal oral temperature for an adult.
A patient is on a "low sodium" diet. Which food item should be avoided?
A) Fresh apple slices
B) Grilled chicken breast
C) Canned soup
D) Steamed rice
Canned and processed foods, like soup, are typically very high in sodium for preservation and flavor. Fresh foods are naturally low in sodium.
The purpose of a clinical practicum is to:
A) Learn new theories about nursing.
B) Apply learned skills in a real-world healthcare setting.
C) Take the final written exam.
D) Observe nurses without participating in care.
The clinical practicum is where students transition from theory to practice, applying their classroom knowledge and skills to care for actual patients under supervision.
If a CNA suspects a coworker is abusing a patient, what is the first thing they should do?
A) Confront the coworker directly.
B) Tell other CNAs about it.
C) Immediately report the suspicion to the charge nurse.
D) Wait to see if it happens again.
All healthcare workers have a legal and ethical duty to report any suspicion of abuse immediately through the proper chain of command to ensure patient safety.
